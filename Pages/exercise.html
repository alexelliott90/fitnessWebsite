Exercise

Exercise is important for health and is proven to lead to a better quality of life

This page is divided into my two key pillars to exercise: Weights and Cardio

Weights

My favourite weights exercises

Deadlift

This is a big lift, so form is very important. Load a barbell with the appropriate weight for your fitness level (start light to begin with). Start with the weight on the floor, with your hips lower than your back, stand up and bring the weight to your hips (with your arms straight) and then reverse the movement and place it back on the floor. Bench Press

Head to a bench in your gym, and load a barbell with the appropriate weight for your fitness level (start light to begin with). Lie on the bench and then press the bar upwards until your arms are fully extended, and then bring the bar back down again.

Save for later

Dips

Line up your arms on the dip bars so that they are in line with your shoulders, then push your body up until your arms are straight and then come back down again until your shoulders are in line with your elbows, and then repeat.

Cardio

My favourite cardio exercises

Running

Running is an excellent way to build cardiovascular fitness, especially as you can do it with minimum equipment. If you are new to running, start with short distances (around 1km) and build up the distance each time you go running. Always have some recovery time between runs (at least a couple of days when you start). A great way to motivate yourself is to join an event and then train for it - for example, you could aim to run in your local parkrun, which is a 5km free run every saturday morning at hundreds of locations in the UK and abroad.

Cycling

Cycling is another great way to build cardio fitness, and can help to build muscle in other parts of your legs that running does not. This is important to prevent injury. You dont need to have the most expensive bike in the world - just a basic bike is good to start out with, and then just get out into your local area and explore whilst building your fitness.
