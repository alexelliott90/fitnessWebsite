Nutrition

Nutrition is as important for health as exercise, will improve the effects of your exercise

There are two parts to your nutrition - Food and Drink!

Food

Food is not just about calories. A balanced diet with plenty of fresh food will improve the effects of your exercise, and improve your gut health. Eat plenty of fruit and veg, proteins, carbohydrates and 'good fats'

Personally, I avoid processed protein shakes and other 'health supplements' as these are full of additives and in the past have led me to put on weight. The best thing to fuel your exercise is good carbonhydrate and natural sources of protein!

Drink

What you drink is equally as important as what you eat. Avoiding processed drinks is a good idea - such as sweetened fizzy drinks and processed fruit juice. Drink plenty of water before, during and after exercise, and avoid energy drinks. Coffee and tea are known to have some positive health impacts, but avoid too much caffeine!

It is common to drink alcohol, and I do enjoy beer and wine as a treat - but be careful as too much alcohol will likely lead to weight gain and will reduce the effect of your exercise!
